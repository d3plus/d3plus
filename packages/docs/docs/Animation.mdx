# Animation & Motion Trails

D3plus animates changes for you. When a chart's data or configuration updates —
including when a timeline advances — shapes tween smoothly between states rather
than snapping. This page covers the animation controls and **motion trails**.

## Transition duration

Every visualization eases between renders over a configurable `duration`
(milliseconds). Set it to `0` to disable animation:

```jsx
<BarChart config={{data, groupBy: "region", x: "quarter", y: "revenue", duration: 600}} />
```

Text animates too: a label whose font-size changes between renders grows or
shrinks into place instead of jumping.

## Motion trails

When a mark moves between timeline frames (press **play**, or step the timeline),
it sweeps a **motion trail** — a tapering cone from its previous position to the
current one, colored with the mark's own color and fading to transparent at the
tail. Trails make the direction and speed of movement legible, Hans Rosling-style.

Trails are **on by default** for scatter/bubble points and Geomap coordinate
points, and they work identically on the SVG and Canvas backends.

```jsx
<Plot config={{
  data,                    // rows with a group id, a time field, and x/y
  groupBy: "id",
  time: "year",
  x: "x", y: "y",
  size: "value"
  // shapeConfig.Circle.trail is true by default
}} />
```

- `Circle` and `Rect` marks both trail. A square's cone is sized to its silhouette
  *perpendicular to travel*, so a diagonal move reaches corner-to-corner rather
  than edge-to-edge.
- Opt out per chart with `shapeConfig.Circle.trail: false` (or `Rect.trail`).

See the **Timeline Motion Trails**, **Square Motion Trails**, and **Persistent
Motion Trails** examples on the [Plot](/?path=/docs/core-charts-plot--docs) page,
and the point-trail example on the [Geomap](/?path=/docs/core-charts-geomap--docs)
page.

## Persistent trails

By default a trail shows only the current move and fades on arrival.
`shapeConfig.Circle.trailPersist` keeps past moves visible too:

```jsx
<Plot config={{
  data, groupBy: "id", time: "year", x: "x", y: "y",
  shapeConfig: {Circle: {trailPersist: true}}
}} />
```

- A **number** keeps that many step-segments; **`true`** keeps a long, slowly
  fading snail-trail.
- Trails follow the timeline's direction — they grow **forward** and **rewind**
  (newest segment first) when you step back — and a multi-period jump traces
  through the skipped periods rather than cutting a straight line.
- The whole trail draws as a single shape, so overlapping turns don't darken.

Setting `trailPersist` is all you need: it automatically switches the chart to a
single-period timeline (`brushing: false`) and fixed axes (`axisPersist: true`) —
the two conditions a persistent trail needs to stay coherent.
